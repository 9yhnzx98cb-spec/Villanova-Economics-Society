About Us

Who we are

A community of Villanova students who believe economics is best learned through conversation, research, and engagement with the world beyond the classroom.

Our Mission

The Villanova Economics Society exists to deepen students' engagement with economics as both an academic discipline and a way of understanding the world. We host speaker events, lead reading groups, support undergraduate research, and connect students with alumni working across finance, consulting, policy, and academia.

We believe that the best economic thinking happens at the intersection of theory and practice — where rigorous analysis meets real questions about how markets, institutions, and people behave. Our programming is designed to bring those questions to life.

What We Offer

Speaker series. We bring economists, policymakers, and industry professionals to campus for talks, fireside chats, and Q&A sessions. Past topics have included monetary policy, labor markets, AI and the economy, and the political economy of trade.

Discussion groups. Smaller, conversational gatherings where members debate current economic events, work through influential papers, and share perspectives across class years and majors.

Research opportunities. We help connect interested members to faculty-led research projects in the Department of Economics, and provide a forum for students to present their own work.

Career and professional development. Resume workshops, interview prep, and networking events with alumni working in economics-adjacent careers — including consulting, banking, government, and graduate study.

Who Should Join

Every Villanova student. While many of our members are Economics majors or minors, we actively welcome students from the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences, the Villanova School of Business, the College of Engineering, and the College of Nursing. If you're curious about how the world works, you belong here.

There are no dues, no GPA requirement, and no application — just show up, engage, and contribute.
